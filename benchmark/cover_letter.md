I am pleased to submit our manuscript entitled **"RA-PainKG: Construction, Coverage-Gap Documentation, and Benchmark Validation of a Tissue-Contextualized Knowledge Graph for Rheumatoid Arthritis Pain Signaling"** for consideration for publication in *Database: The Journal of Biological Databases and Curation*.

**Summary of Contribution**

This manuscript presents RA-PainKG, a tissue-contextualized knowledge graph that fills a specific gap in the biomedical database landscape: a resource that integrates curated pain gene annotations with multimodal biomedical data, systematically documents knowledge gaps, and provides transparent benchmark validation.

Three contributions are of particular relevance to the *Database* readership:

1. **Resource construction with systematic gap documentation.** RA-PainKG integrates PrimeKG v1.0, GTEx v8 tissue expression (54 tissues), and manual curation of nine pain signaling pathways into a dual-track framework (Track A: immune-inflammation, Track B: nociception). Critically, we document 72 core pain genes (37.5%) absent from PrimeKG-a transparency practice that is uncommon in biomedical database publications but essential for honest resource development.

2. **Open, reusable formats.** The graph is provided in GraphML, CSV, and Python pickle formats with complete construction source code under the MIT license. The modular pipeline enables re-generation with updated input resources.

3. **Transparent benchmark validation with boundary conditions.** Validation across 11 KG variants establishes honest performance characterization rather than inflated claims, including explicit documentation of test system limitations (K562 cells express only 26.7% of pain genes).

We believe this manuscript aligns with *Database*'s scope as a resource paper that combines rigorous construction methodology, systematic coverage analysis, and transparent validation.
